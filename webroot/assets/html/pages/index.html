about us

VEX Robotics is an international robotics competition in which teams design, build, and program their own robots from officially sanctioned components and compete in tournaments for qualification for the next level. Regional tournaments qualify for state and national championships; from there, teams compete for spots at the VEX World Championship in Louisville, KY.

We're team 2105 from Ware County High School in Waycross, Georgia. We've competed in VEX Robotics since 2010, won several tournaments, earned many awards, and most importantly, had a lot of fun. We are competitive, organized, motivated, and hard-working. We're constantly developing our hardware, software, and strategy.

While our school's Project Lead the Way engineering program is a significant source of new members, we do not have class time to work on robots. Our members work mostly after school and on weekends.

Ultimately, VEX Robotics is all about learning, exploring, inventing, and having fun, and we're glad to have the opportunity to do that.

process flow

As engineering students, our #1 Rule when creating alternative solutions is to not settle for the first idea. During this phase, we try to generate as many possible solutions as we can before choosing one that we feel is the best. This creative process of developing ideas is called ideation. The key to brainstorming is to remember that no idea is a bad idea! Each idea, no matter how off-the-wall it seems, should be recorded during the brainstorming session. The more ideas that are generated, the more likely it is that a successful design will result.
